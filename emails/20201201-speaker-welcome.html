Thank you for submitting a talk to BarCamp Philly 2020, happening this Saturday, December 5, 2020.

Join us on Discord!

You are confirmed as a speaker for the event, and are receiving the first invites this week to the Discord space. This link expires in 24 hours:

Join BarCamp Philly Discord

If you already have a Discord account and app, this link will open up the app and bring you into the server. If you don't have a Discord account yet, the link will let you download the app or sign-up via the browser experience on desktop or laptop devices.

Once you get into Discord, you will find your talk's channel

This will be where we hope all of the chat can and will happen during your talk. The presentation itself will happen in Zoom, but we are directing all chat to Discord. It is a great place to put any material that your attendees might want to read or look at when they arrive in the “room.”

Since the posts will remain, you can pin important content to the channel, and you, your attendees, and moderator can drop ideas, links, questions, as the talk goes and then after it has ended. You can also chat with organizers and volunteers in #speaker-chat and BarCampers will have access to post in #forum to make introductions and chat.

At BarCamp, your talk will happen in Zoom

Your talk will have a dedicated moderator volunteer, and the criteria you supplied in your submission form (no recording, muting attendees, etc.) will be available to you and your moderator to confirm.

Your room will become available between 15 and 10 minutes before your talk time, so we will encourage you day-of to arrive early to make sure you are good to go!

After your talk, take it back to Discord

We also encourage speakers–if you are in a conversation as your time slot is ending that you want to continue–to bring their group to a voice channel, where you can all keep the conversation going, while allowing the next speaker to start in the Zoom room.

Want to do a test-run this week?

We are hosting volunteer and organizer-run test sessions this week, Tuesday through Friday, for speakers who want to ensure they know what to expect and that their hardware and software is working.
